## The Philosophy

### Power Requires Restraint

The fact that we CAN do something doesn't mean we SHOULD.

**With great capability comes great responsibility:**
- Use skills for legitimate purposes only
- Stay within legal boundaries
- Don't be tempted by "easy money"
- Don't rationalize vigilante actions

### The Test

Before using this capability, ask:
1. **Do we have explicit permission?** (puzzle creator, own wallet, legal authority)
2. **Is this legal?** (not just "morally justified")
3. **Would we defend this in court?** (with lawyers and prosecutors)
4. **What's the worst case?** (prison vs. reward - is it worth it?)

If any answer is unclear = DON'T DO IT.

## The Scam Wallet Case

### Why We DON'T Touch It

**bc1qxy2kgdygjrsqtzq2n0yrf2493p83kkfjhx0wlh:**
- ~0.19 BTC current balance
- ~$8,000 at current prices
- Scammed from innocent victims in 2020

**Why not "recover" it?**

1. **Legal:** 
   - No authority to seize funds
   - Would be theft under law
   - Scam victims have legal claims
   - FBI/law enforcement may be tracking

2. **Ethical:**
   - Funds belong to victims, not us
   - No right to decide distribution
   - Vigilante justice is still wrong
   - Creates precedent for abuse

3. **Practical:**
   - Tainted funds = legal liability
   - Could become targets ourselves
   - Not worth legal risk
   - Impossible to "do good" illegally

### What We DO Instead

**Report to authorities:**
- FBI IC3 (Internet Crime Complaint Center)
- Blockchain analysis firms
- Let legal system handle it

**Document methodology:**
- Share security research
- Help improve wallet security
- Educate on vulnerabilities
- Publish findings responsibly
